Natural Patterns

The concept of natural patterns links three of my main interests: philosophy, rock climbing, and computer programming or software engineering.

One similarity is that in each discipline, there is often a natural way through a problem. This way can be novel, unexpected, and even controversial; but the common examples always combine elegance and effectiveness.

I studied philosophy as an undergraduate, continuing through graduate school (in Scotland and at Notre Dame), then going back to the United Kingdom for four years as a member of the philosophy faculty at the University of Nottingham.

After returning to the States, I became interested in rock climbing through films like 180 South and To the Limit. However, it was a couple of years before I actually went to a climbing center -- EarthTreks -- in late 2014. Since then I have been back almost every weekend and frequently during the week as well.

My interest in programming is somewhat similar, in that I've had this interest for several years, without successfully pursuing and developing it. But after reading an interesting book in fall 2015 -- John Kotter's Leading Change -- I am pursuing programming with more urgency, and with a better understanding of the process, and the possibilities.
