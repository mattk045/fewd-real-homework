Baseball

At my job and in other circles I am known as a big baseball fan. Although sometimes I feel a bit ambivalent about this, it is certainly correct.

About 12 years ago, Michael Lewis wrote a book called Moneyball, which portrayed the increasing use of novel statistics within baseball to change our understanding of the game, and to change how decisions were made about players, and about tactics within games themselves. This opened an interesting door, but I didn't really go through it until a few years ago, when I moved back to Washington and started closely following the Washington Nationals.

Over the last few years, I have learned a lot more about new ways of analyzing the game, and watched a huge number of Nationals games. I have even written a couple of posts at FanGraphs and on my own (intermittent) baseball blog.
